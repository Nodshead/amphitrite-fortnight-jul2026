Steamship Mutual

P&I · 14:00
Taslim Imad · Manager, Loss Prevention · Master Mariner, MBA, AFNI · 155 Bishopsgate
The door: he came to us, replying to the London insurance campaign that reconstructing the conditions a vessel actually experienced "sounds interesting".
  • Claims-side reports landed as the wedge: an independent account of the weather along a voyage, from a model that was already running when it happened, plus the route re-enactment showing safest course against charter speed.
  • He was invited to shape the report format to how their desks actually work; tailoring is the pitch, not a fixed product.
  • Preventative lane redirected: for live safeguards he pointed us at OrcaAI territory; useful honesty about where loss prevention buys versus where claims buys.
  • Credibility angle that worked: the same sea-state models run in defence and anti-submarine work; evidence that holds for navies holds in disputes.
Where it stands: recap and open questions sent same day, 16:18, with a technical call with our team as the proposed next step. No reply yet; a follow-up goes out early next week.

6 Learnings

What I am keeping

i

Out-of-office replies are doors, not dead ends

23 OOO auto-replies came back this window. Each names who is covering; the redirect carries the original note to that person with an honest first line. The Hiscox meeting exists because of one: Ben Watson's auto-reply named Kevin Lee, and five days later we were having coffee at 22 Bishopsgate.

ii

Third touches are where replies live

75 threads received their third or later email this window, 18 of them Breakbulk. The pattern held: Roberto introduced SeaCras's CEO on touch three; Fabio moved on touch two and again on three. Persistence works when each touch adds substance instead of repeating the last one.

iii

Our own press release became outreach ammunition

We put out the Sleipnir story and four trade outlets carried it. "A few outlets picked it up this week, Splash247 tells it best" is a different sentence from a cold claim: it signals initiative, and it directly triggered the SeaCras introduction.

iv

The insurance pitch sharpened in the rooms

Both desks bought the same wedge: an independent record of conditions that existed before the loss, not an expert report built after it. The preventative lane routes differently: loss prevention pointed toward the ops side (and named OrcaAI territory), so claims and prevention get pitched as separate doors. A TMK energy underwriter added a segment idea worth a probe: marine warranty surveyors, who already handle sea-state work for the upstream desks.
